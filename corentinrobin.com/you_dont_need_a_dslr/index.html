YOU PROBABLY DON'T NEED A DSLR.

Author: Corentin ROBIN ; corentin.robin@gmail.com

Introduction

As an amateur photographer for almost 4 years, I've been using a DSLR(1) all the time.
Then I noticed that a lot of picture I took and I really liked were taken with my phone, and I noticed that I didn't take my gear with me as much as before.
So I decided to go on the Internet and look for alternatives, and discovered that compact cameras nowadays give equal result (image quality wise) to DSLRs.
So I bought one(2) and started to use it almost daily (super convenient as it fits in my pocket).
For my test, the only differences I noticed were a loss of sharpness in the corners of the pictures, and slightly less details when zooming in on my computer, issues I didn't have with my DSLR, but looking both identical on full screen (13" retina display).
So the question is: as the pictures looks exactly the same, and if you don't have to sell or give massive prints, why on Earth would you buy a DSLR?
(Note that given the retina display pixel density, if it looks good on it, you can definitely print it, no problems.)

People who need high-end gear

Wildlife photographer

A wildlife photographer needs a long focal length, to avoid scaring animals and make them run away.
He needs a big aperture and a big sensor, as he will be often working in low light condition (often in woodlands).
See: Morten Hilmer, Vincent Munier

Sport photographer

He needs a high shutter speed, so a big aperture took capture as much light as possible during this short exposure.
He needs a long focal length, because the action is far away (tennisman, football player, F1 car)

Landscape photographer

He will need a good optical quality and a big sensor if he want to sell prints for example.
See: Thomas Heaton

Reportage photographer

He will need a big aperture and a big sensor if he wants to be able to capture any action at any time of the day, he will be often in low light conditions.

Us, the amateurs

In my personal experience, I can take whatever shot in whatever condition, my 1" sensor is enough in low light condition, and the 25 to 250mm is way enough to frame whatever scene I want.
And if I want to record something, it can record 4K as well.
This kind of camera costs around 450€ in 2021, the equivalent in DSLR costs around 800€ (body + zoom lens).
I concluded that I didn't need a DSLR anymore, and you probably don't either.

Conclusion

I think 90% of amateur photographers don't need a DSLR, and I think in 2021 we can even do photography only with a phone (you will have only a limited zoom though).
Most of people having a full frame camera will post their images only on Instagram, and those pictures will be watched mostly from a phone...
Note that most of medium range compact cameras and some recent phones allow you to shoot RAW.
